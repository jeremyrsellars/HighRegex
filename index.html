Higher-order Regular Expressions for .Net

It is safe to say that one of the most well-known declarative languages is Regex.

So, what is a higher-order Regex?

This speaks to the complexity of the data and of the expression.

Let's consider classic Regex.

The classic Regex is a great way to find subsequences of characters that match what you're looking for.

Their use is simple (if seldom easy): you declare what you're looking for in a sequence of characters (string).

  • abc matches the literal characters a followed by b followed by c in `abcdefghijkl...'
  • gr[ea]y matches either grey or gray - g followed by r followed by an e or an a followed by y
  • baa+ matches baa, baaa, baaaa, etc.
  • \d matches a single digit character
  • \d+\.\d* matches one or more digit characters, followed by ., followed by 0 or more digits
